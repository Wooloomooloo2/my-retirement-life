How to read this

Success rate. The percentage of the {{ mc.n_sims }} simulated paths in which your total balance stays above zero every year through to life expectancy. A 70% success rate means 30% of paths exhausted savings before life expectancy.

Shock model. Each year the same market shock is applied across all of your investment accounts, drawn from a normal distribution per the selected scenario profile (Conservative / Moderate / Aggressive). Cash interest is deterministic — the volatility you see comes from investments only.

Why MC and the deterministic projection can disagree. The deterministic projection assumes you earn the average return every year, in order. Monte Carlo reshuffles that same average across volatile years — and a poor sequence of returns early in retirement (sequence-of-returns risk) drains accounts faster than the average alone implies. A green deterministic outcome paired with a low MC success rate is the model telling you the plan survives on average but is fragile to bad luck.
