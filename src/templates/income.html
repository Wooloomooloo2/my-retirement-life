How the deposit account affects your projection

When set to an account that is not the projection's spending account, the year's income is credited directly to the chosen account and is not recognised as covering this year's drawdown — the engine still pulls from your drawdown waterfall to cover spending, even when the income has landed somewhere with cash to spare.

When the deposit account matches the spending account (configured on the Projection page, or the first current/cash account when not yet configured), the income is treated as covering that year's spending first; only the surplus stays on the balance. This matches the usual "salary lands in current account, bills come out of current account" mental model.

Match the deposit account to your drawdown priority. For routed income that is not the spending account, the engine still pulls from the drawdown waterfall — so route income to a high-priority account (small drawdown-priority number — drawn early) so the engine spends it before depleting other accounts. Routing income to a drawn-last account (e.g. priority 999) lets it compound there while other accounts drain faster — on a long horizon the divergence can run into the millions.

Leave unset for the simplest behaviour: income offsets that year's spending and only the shortfall (if any) is drawn from your accounts.
